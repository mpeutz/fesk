Specificity Graph

Harry Roberts first suggested creating a Specificity Graph to get a better picture of your CSS.

The Specificity Graph works by taking a look at your entire projectâ€™s CSS, and plotting the specificity of all of its selectors on a simple line graph. On the x axis we plot Location in Stylesheet and on the y axis we plot the specificity of that selector.

In general the graph should progress from lower to higher with few spikes occurring at the end. Spikes that occur mid-graph could lead to maintainability issues since CSS that occurs afterwards needs to meet or exceed that level of specificity to override earlier CSS.
